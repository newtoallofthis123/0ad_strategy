Four green lights to attack

  1. You out-eco them +15 villagers. Convert eco lead into military.
  2. Their army is on the wrong side of the map (raiding someone else).
  3. You have siege + they have no walls. Walk in, break it, walk out.
  4. They just lost a fight. Don't let them rebuild — push now.

The far-base attack — your nemesis

Decide in 5 seconds:
Army I can send now > their attacking army? → COMMIT EVERYTHING.
Army I can send now < theirs? → WRITE OFF. COUNTER-ATTACK THEIR BASE.
Never: "I'll send some to slow them down."

Order of operations

  1. March whole army to ONE target — usually their secondary base.
  2. Kill the buildings, then retreat. Don't get into an attrition fight.
  3. Wait 5 minutes. Their main is now starved of that base's income.
  4. Push the main. Siege breaks walls, pikes hold their counter, archers kill workers.
// Pitfall

Do not push the main first. Their main has the most defenders. Take limbs before you take the heart.

PTOL.01 Mercenaries // Pizza Delivery CIV SIGNATURE

The reframe

Mercenaries are NOT elite veterans you must protect. They are military teleportation, paid for in metal. They trade food/wood cost → metal cost, in return for fast spawning and forward placement.

MANTRA
Mercenaries are pizza delivery, not knights. Use them. Lose them. Reorder them.

Five times to use them

  1. Forward Reinforcements — camp near the fight. Units spawn 10s from front, not 60s.
  2. Emergency Defense — attack incoming, drop camp at the choke, pump instantly.
  3. Metal-Rich Eco — late game, metal overflowing, food tight. Mercs spend the right resource.
  4. Filling Comp Gaps — need slingers fast, home barracks full? Merc camp covers it.
  5. Out-of-Position — army fighting far from home. Drop a camp halfway across the map.

When NOT to use

  • Phase I (no metal yet)
  • Food overflowing, metal tight
  • As your main bulk army — citizens are cheaper

The signature move: Forward Merc Camp

10:00 villager forward 10:30 merc camp halfway 11:00 camp active 11:30 reinforce in 10s

Under cover of your standing army, build a Merc Camp halfway to the enemy. Your forward outpost is now a unit factory. Reinforcements for any attack spawn in 10 seconds. Devastating on Medium AI — the AI doesn't handle forward production well.

PTOL.03 Champions // The Hammer PHASE III · 5-10 MAX

The rule

Champions are force multipliers, not bulk. 5-10 in your army, never 30. Math: a champion costs 3-4× a citizen but is only ~2× as strong. 10 champions + 30 citizens beats 50 citizens — because the champions create the line-break the citizens exploit.

The Ptolemies roster

ChampionRoleWhen to build
Champion Pikeman (Machimoi)Flagship. Line-breaker, anti-cav, anti-elephant. Front rank.First. Always.
Champion Infantry / SwordHeavy melee. Breaks enemy pike walls.If facing pike-heavy enemy.
Champion CavalryElite cav for decisive flank. Not for raids.For the decisive push only.
War ElephantsTank + building crusher. Champion-tier role.Phase III, with champion pikes as escort.

Army layering with champions

FRONT champion pikes 2ND regular pikes 3RD archers + slingers WINGS cav REAR elephants + siege
DON'T
Don't hoard them (10 champions unused < 10 champions used). Don't waste them on raids (200 metal = 1 tower or 3 upgrades). Use them as the hammer; let citizens take the bulk of losses.

The signature late-game stack

Champion Pikemen + War Elephants = the Ptolemies endgame composition. Elephants tank, champion pikes shred any cav that tries to flank, mass ranged behind does the killing. Very few clean counters before minute 20.

PTOL.04 Heroes // The Aura ONE OF EACH · KEEP ALIVE

What heroes really are

Unique named historical figures, trained at the Fortress in Phase III. Only one of each at a time. The real value isn't their combat — it's the aura, a passive buff to every friendly unit within ~30 tiles.

FUNDAMENTAL RULE
KEEP THEM ALIVE. A dead hero = no aura = ~15-20% army strength gone instantly. Cost + training time is massive. They are NOT champions. They cannot be lost casually.

The three Ptolemies heroes

HeroBuffsPick when
👑 Ptolemy I SoterInfantry attack & armorStandard pike+sword army
🐘 Ptolemy IV PhilopatorCavalry & elephants (he rides an elephant himself)Elephant-heavy late game
💰 Cleopatra VIIEconomic / training speed (varies by patch)Long boom, eco focus

Where to put the hero

Center of your army formation. Not the front (he dies). Not the back (aura doesn't reach the front line). Middle, surrounded by ~10 escort units. Move the hero, the aura moves with him.

Build timing

18:00 phase III + first champions 19:00 build/locate fortress 20:00 train hero (~60-90s) 21:00 assign 5-10 escorts 21:30 position center of army

Common mistakes

  • ❌ Sending hero alone to scout → dies to 3 spearmen
  • ❌ Treating like a champion — heroes are not expendable
  • ❌ Building all three at once — pick the one matching your army
  • ❌ Building too early — Phase III, after you have an army
  • ❌ Forgetting where he is in a chaotic fight

The endgame trio

Ptolemy IV (on elephant) + 2 War Elephants + 8 Champion Pikemen = the Ptolemies signature endgame stack. Elephants tank, champions hold, hero's aura buffs the whole stack ~15%, Ptolemy IV himself rides in. Roll this with citizen + ranged support and you win most engagements.

REF.02 Formations // Four Shapes /

// PHALANX

Pikes front, archers behind. Hold ground, defend chokes. Default formation.

// WEDGE

Cavalry only. Breaks through ranged lines. For raids and flanking pikes.

// BOX

Pikes face out, ranged inside. Surrounded? Outnumbered? Box up and survive.

// SKIRMISH

Spread wide, throw, retreat, repeat. Counter slow melee blobs. Never melee.
