ECONOMY+UPGRADES

FLOW NOT STOCKPILE SPEND OR BLEED UPGRADES ARE PROFIT MERCS ARE PIZZA
DOCTRINE Three Laws of Economy READ FIRST
LAW 01 — Economy is FLOW, not STOCKPILE. A bank of 2000 wood is a frozen economy. Income → spending → income → spending. Don't hoard.
LAW 02 — Every upgrade is profit. Most pay back in 60-90 seconds. Not researching them = leaving free resources on the table every minute.
LAW 03 — Upgrades only matter if you have units to benefit. Buy bodies before you buy boosts. 3 units with a buff < 6 units without.
REF.01 The Four Resources EACH HAS A JOB

Resources aren't interchangeable. Each does a specific job. Knowing which you need shapes what you build.

FOOD
/ GROWTH
Villagers, basic infantry, farms. Your growth resource. Constantly draining.
WOOD
/ INFRASTRUCTURE
Buildings, ranged units, ships, outposts. Your infrastructure resource.
STONE
/ DEFENSE
Walls, towers, mining camps. Your defense resource. Underused by beginners.
METAL
/ TECH + ELITE
Champions, upgrades, advanced units, siege. Your tech resource. Phase III drinks this.

ROI The Upgrade Priority Ladder RESEARCH IN ORDER

Most upgrades pay themselves back in 60-90 seconds. After that = pure profit. Skipping them is leaving free resources on the table.

This is the order to research. Top = highest ROI.

  • 01
    Phase-ups — Phase II at ~min 6, Phase III at ~min 15. Unlocks everything.
    ALWAYS
  • 02
    Resource gathering rate — wood/stone/metal +X% from storage/farmstead. ROI in <90s.
    ALWAYS
  • 03
    Blacksmith Weapon +1/+2/+3 — affects EVERY combat unit. Every game, every tier.
    ALWAYS
  • 04
    Blacksmith Armor +1/+2/+3 — same as weapon. Stacks with everything.
    ALWAYS
  • 05
    Barracks pike upgrades — if you have 15+ pikes (you do).
    USUALLY
  • 06
    Civ-unique bonus techs — Ptolemies has good ones (farming, mercenaries). Look at your bonus tree.
    USUALLY
  • 07
    Stable-specific cav upgrades — only if you have 8+ of that cav type. Below that, buy bodies instead.
    CONDITIONAL
  • 08
    Champion-specific upgrades — only after you've actually built champions in numbers.
    CONDITIONAL
  • 09
    Naval upgrades — only on water maps.
    SKIP
The math: Blacksmith +1 weapon on a 40-unit army > stable spear-cav upgrade on 3 cav. The wider your upgrade reaches, the higher the ROI.
MERCS Mercenaries // Pizza Delivery NOT KNIGHTS

// The Reframe

Mercenaries are NOT elite veterans you must protect. They are military teleportation, paid for in metal. They trade food/wood cost → metal cost, and in return give you fast spawning and forward placement.

Mantra: Mercenaries are pizza delivery, not knights. Use them. Lose them. Reorder them.

// Five times to use them

  1. Forward Reinforcements — build a Merc Camp near the fight. Units spawn 10s from the front instead of 60s.
  2. Emergency Defense — attack incoming, drop a camp at the choke, pump units instantly.
  3. Metal-Rich Eco — late game, you have 600 metal and food can't keep up. Mercs spend metal instead.
  4. Filling Composition Gaps — need slingers fast, home barracks full? Merc camp handles it.
  5. Out-of-Position Reinforcement — army fighting far from home, drop a camp halfway across the map.

// When NOT to use

  • Phase I (no metal yet)
  • Food overflowing, metal tight
  • As your main bulk army — citizens are cheaper

// The Ptolemies signature: Forward Merc Camp

Around minute 10-12:

  1. Send 1 villager to a spot halfway to the enemy
  2. Build a Mercenary Camp under cover of your standing army
  3. Now your forward outpost is also a unit factory
  4. Reinforcements for any attack spawn in 10 seconds

This is the Ptolemies signature move. Devastating on Medium AI — the AI doesn't handle forward production well.

EXPAND Second CC // When + Where DOUBLE THE ECO

A second Civic Centre gives you: a second source of villager production, a second food-gathering hub, vision & map control.

// The rule

Drop your second CC when Phase II completes, on the closest unclaimed resource cluster — usually stone or metal.

// One CC is fine for Easy. You NEED two for Medium and up.

Reason: a single CC can only produce villagers at one queue. Medium AI runs two queues. You need to match production or fall behind on workers.

// Don't drop a CC if

  • You can't defend it (no walls/towers within 30s walk)
  • It's in the open with no natural choke
  • You're under attack right now (build a fort first, expand later)

ENDPLATE Economy Habits MEMORIZE
  1. Economy is flow. Spend the moment you cross a tripwire.
  2. Women on food. Men on wood/stone/metal. Never mix.
  3. Standing army never gathers. Working soldiers are reserves.
  4. Always queue villagers in the CC. Always. Until you have 70.
  5. Two CCs by minute 12. One queue is not enough.
  6. Phase II at 25 vil. Phase III at 50-60. Not earlier, not later.
  7. Always research Phase-ups, gather techs, blacksmith. Free profit.
  8. Mercenaries are pizza delivery. Use the forward camp.
