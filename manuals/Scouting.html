SCOUTING/EYES

SEE BEFORE THEY SEE YOU ASK A QUESTION FIRST DEATH IS DATA OUTPOSTS DON'T DIE
DOCTRINE Three Laws of Scouting READ FIRST
LAW 01 — A scout's job is information, not combat. If it's fighting, it's failed.
LAW 02 — Scout WITH a question, not "to look around." A specific question changes where you send the scout.
LAW 03 — The map is uncontested in Phase I. Build outposts before you need them. Once the map heats up, you can't sneak out to place them.
PRIMER What Is A Scout DEFINITION

A scout is any unit you send to gather information instead of fight. Ptolemies start with a camel — that IS your scout. Most civs start with one cavalry unit for this purpose.

The intelligence you're after:

  • Where is the enemy base?
  • Where is their army right now?
  • What units are they building?
  • Are they teching / expanding / preparing to attack?
  • What resources are they on?
The information gap is the strategic gap. A blind player loses to a slightly weaker player with vision.
TOOL 01 Cavalry Scouting ★ EARLY GAME · FAST

// The Tool

Send your starting cav on a wide loop around the map. It's fast, free, and reveals huge amounts of map in the first 4 minutes.

// When it works

Phase I (0-6 min). The map is empty. No one has built units yet. Your cav can ride right past their CC and live.

// When it stops working

When the enemy has units patrolling — usually by minute 8-10. Your cav starts dying.

The rule: Always keep ONE cav alive on patrol. If it dies, replace it within 30 seconds. The fog-of-war is what's killing your far bases.
TOOL 02 Outposts & Watchtowers ★★ MID + LATE · CAN'T DIE

// The Tool

50 wood per outpost. Permanent vision in a circle around them. They can be killed, but only with effort — usually requiring siege.

// Where to place

  • Hills & ridges (extended sight)
  • Map edges (rarely patrolled)
  • Choke points NEAR enemy approach
  • Neutral resource deposits (denies + scouts)

// Critical timing

Build them in Phase I-II, while the map is uncontested. Once the enemy roams, you can't sneak villagers out to place them.

The single biggest scouting fix: 4 outposts by minute 8. Cost: 200 wood. Value: permanent vision for the rest of the game.
TOOL 03 Edge Probes & Sacrifices ★★★ LATE GAME · DEATH IS DATA

// The Tool

Send cheap throwaway units along multiple paths simultaneously. Some will die. Where they die tells you where the enemy army is.

// How it works

  1. Send 3 cav (or spearmen) along 3 different paths — top edge, middle, bottom edge
  2. Map edges are rarely patrolled — those usually survive
  3. If the middle one dies, the enemy army is along the middle
  4. You learned 3 paths of info for the cost of 1 cheap unit

// The mindset

A dead scout is not a wasted scout. Where it died is the intel. Death = data.

Use this when you can't get a live scout close. Late game, this is often your only option.
METHOD Scout WITH A Question PREDICTIVE

Beginners scout randomly. Pros scout with a specific question they're trying to answer. The question changes WHERE you send the scout, WHEN, and what you're looking at.

// Is their army still at home?
Send cav toward their CC. If it dies fast → army is home. If it reaches the CC → army is out somewhere else.
// Are they teching to Phase III?
Look at their CC for the phase-up upgrade animation. Or scan for new building shapes: Forts, Embassies, Wonders.
// Did they expand to that resource?
Patrol the natural expansion points — stone, metal, second CC spots. Workers there = they expanded.
// Are they about to attack?
Look for: army gathering at chokepoints, forward outposts being built, tower being placed near you.
// What's their army composition?
Sneak cav close enough to see units, then retreat. You only need 2 seconds of vision. Note ratios, retreat.
// Where is the weak point I should attack?
Patrol around their base — find the side with fewest towers, no walls, exposed workers.
The shift: from "I should scout" to "I need to know X — let me confirm." Specific question, specific tool, specific time.
PHASES Scouting By Phase WHAT TO USE WHEN

// PHASE I

0-6 MIN

Cavalry loop. Map is uncontested. One cav, ride wide circle. Find enemy CC by minute 4. Build first 2 outposts at map edges.

// PHASE II

6-12 MIN

Cav + outposts. Cav still works but riskier. Build 2 more outposts at chokes. Patrol cav stays alive — replaces if killed.

// PHASE III+

12 MIN — END

Outposts + edge probes. Cav can't get close anymore. Read from your outposts. Send sacrificial probes when you need fresh data.

ENDPLATE The Scouting Habit MEMORIZE
  1. Minute 1: Start your cav loop. Find enemy by minute 4.
  2. Minute 6: Build outposts 1-2 at safe map edges.
  3. Minute 10: Build outposts 3-4 at chokepoints near approaches.
  4. Every 60 seconds: ask one specific question. Send a scout to answer it.
  5. When cav stops surviving: switch to edge probes + outpost reading.
  6. If you ever lose vision: immediately replace the dead scout. Don't wait.
  7. Death is data: if a probe dies, you learned where the enemy is.
  8. The information gap is the strategic gap. Vision wins games.
